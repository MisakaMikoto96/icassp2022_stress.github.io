Audio samples from "AUTOMATIC SYLLABLE-LEVEL STRESS ANNOTATION AND MODELING IN SPEECH SYNTHESIS"

Abstract: Current text-to-speech technique has developed to a mature level, and more research interest has been paid to highly expressive and more controllable speech synthesis. Stress detection and modeling in Mandarin TTS system has been verified to be an efficient methodology in the previous study to establish the link between linguistic features and acoustic features. But to label the stress manually is time consuming and it is not a trivial task. In this paper, based on the conception of ``tone aggregation'' linguistic findings, we proposed an automatic syllable-level stress annotation mechanism from audio files for speech data. And based on the labelled data a text-to-speech system is built and tested. Experiments show that, proposed method can consistently predict stress through a linguistic context based front-end module. And speech synthesis systems with proposed stress annotation and modeling method outperforms baseline systems.

Q&A context

 In this part, there are 4 system:
  • Baseline (Baseline acoustic model without stress input)
  • Stress_Predictor (An predictor set in acoustic model to predict stress from F0/energy/duration, and then concat with other embeddings.)
  • Front-end_model (Predict stress from text, and make it to be the condition embedding and concat with other embeddings. )
  • Manual (Annotate stress by annotator, and make it to be the condition embedding and concat with other embeddings.)
we use some question-and-answer contexts from our Q&A datasets to verify whether stress obtained by Baseline, Stress_Predictor, Front-end_model,
Manual could make different performance of acoustic model.
Qustion_1: 张三哪天中午吃了粽子？（Which day did Zhang San eat dumplings at noon?）
Baseline Stress_Predictor Front-end_model
 Manual
Answer_1: 张三「今天」中午吃了粽子。(Today, Zhang San had dumplings for lunch.)
Qustion_2: 我闻到了了糊味，是什么东西烧糊了？（What is burning?）
Answer_2: 是他把「面条」烧糊了。（It's the noodle he scorched.）
Qustion_3: 是哪个花瓶碎了？（Which vase is broken?）
Answer_3: 是「这个」花瓶碎了。（It was this vase that broke.）
